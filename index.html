Sobhan Latifi

AI Enthusiast NZ

I'm Sobhan, born and bred in Iran, and a netizen living on the internet for over two decades. Currently, I am a PhD student at the University of Otago and Canterbury, NZ, working with professor Brendan McCane and Holger Regenbrekht. My focus lies in Human computer interaction (HCI) where I'm geting digital 3D objects off their digital base and blowing a soul into them and make them real life objects. The output shapes can be used in prototypings, catalytic beds, filters, chromatographic media, Heat Exchangers and so on. The mentioned geometries can be used in conjunction with other chemical or physical phenomena to boost the existing tools and applications. Before joining Otago, I worked on Robot design and planning in 2014. In 2015 I started studying computer vision alogorithm and worked on star-based navigation systems. I completed my master studies with the focus on derivative- and integral-free methods (matrix computations) to avoid derivative or integral operations in engineering and neruoscience application's compuatations. I was lucky and honoured to work with my mentors, assistant professor Delkhosh and prof. Mohades in Computer and Math schools at Shahid Beheshti and Amir Kabir universities. In 2018, I simulated Real Estate market with game theory based algorithm, where I worked with professor Yunpeng Zhang from Houston Texas university. Ahora mismo, I'm working on Spatial Data Science applications and techniques for analysing forest data. In my free time, if I find any, I flirt with 3d printers and get them work with different techniques. The task that I'm involved with is direct 3d printing that helps print certain functions directly without futher triangulations and modifications in between. This save practitioners time and energy and is able to print solid volumes.
